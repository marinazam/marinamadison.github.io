Average Hourly Ozon Concentration in California, 2010-2011

This is a project for evaluation of impacts of air quality in California. For now, we're concerned with what causes the buildup of ozone. The research question is whether air with higher ozone concentrations disproportionately affects certain groups of people. For this analysis, we explores the affects of elevation on ozone buildup and whether there is a connection between ozone buildup and household income. This project is a part of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) Specialization taught by UC Davis through Coursera.

The project goal is to create a single page PDF map of California using ArcGIS Layout view that contains the following:

  • A graph of Ozone concentration (Y axis) vs Elevation (X axis) with a trend line.
  • A graph of Household income (Y axis) vs Ozone (X axis) with a trend line.
  • A primary, large map showing Census tracts, dissolved to the approximate county boundaries and symbolized by ozone concentration (equal interval symbol classifier with 6 classes). Overlay the air quality station points on top.
  • A secondary, smaller map showing interpolated ozone surface as a raster.
  • Appropriate map elements, such as a title, legend, north arrow, scale bar (for the primary map), date published, projection, and reserachr's name.

    • Geoprocessing Tools used in ArcMap:

    Extract MultiValues to Points Tool, Join Tool (at the Atribute Table), Extract Multi-values to Point tool, Create TIN Tool (in 3D Analyst, NAD_1983_California_Teale_Albers Projection used), TIN to Raster Tool (in 3D Analyst), Zonal statistics as Table Tool, Graphing Tools, Dissolve Tool (in Data management), Legend, Scale Bar, etc (in Insert), Metadata.
